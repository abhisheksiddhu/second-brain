Palantir doesn’t sell data itself — instead it sells software that helps organizations connect, interpret, and use their own data to make decisions.

### What They Sell – Core Products & Platforms
1) Palantir Gotham
		A platform originally built for government, defense, intelligence, and law enforcement use cases. It integrates large volumes of data — structured and unstructured — and helps analysts find patterns, investigate threats, and coordinate operations. Used by various intelligence agencies and police forces.
 2. Palantir Foundry
		A flexible enterprise data platform for commercial and civil organizations. Helps businesses unify data sources, automate workflows, and derive insights across departments like supply chain, finance, healthcare, etc.
3. Palantir Apollo
		A management layer that supports deployment, updates, and maintenance of Palantir software across diverse environments such as cloud, on-premises, and edge.
4. Palantir Artificial Intelligence Platform (AIP)
		Enables organizations to use and govern large language models (LLMs) and other AI systems in secure, enterprise settings.
5. Other Specialist Offerings
		Skykit: Portable hardware/software bundles for harsh or mobile environments, including drones and satellite connectivity.
		MetaConstellation: Infrastructure for connecting devices and sensors in remote or disconnected environments.  

Additional modules tailored to finance (e.g., trading), industrial operations, and security services.

### What They Actually Do – Capabilities
Palantir’s platforms are fundamentally about data operations and operational intelligence:
- Data Integration & Fusion: Combine siloed data from multiple sources (databases, sensors, logs, documents) into a unified view. This breaks down information silos within large organizations.
- Analysis & Visualization: Powerful tools for querying, exploring, and visualizing complex datasets. Helps analysts spot patterns, trends, and anomalies quickly.
- Decision Support & AI: Uses AI/ML to enhance decision workflows — from predictive analytics to operational simulations. The AI Platform lets companies run and control modern LLMs in secure settings.
- Operational Intelligence: Focuses on real-world decisions rather than pure statistics — e.g., in defense, logistics, healthcare response.

  

The system augments human analysts, not just reports numbers.

  

  

What’s Unique About Palantir

  

1. Focus on Operational Use Cases

  

Unlike many analytics firms that provide dashboards or BI tools, Palantir aims to be an operational system — used directly in decision contexts like military planning or enterprise operations.

  

2. Deep Government & Mission-Critical Roots

  

Its initial success came from helping U.S. intelligence and security agencies connect disparate data — and it remains widely used by governments and defense sectors.

  

3. Data Control & Security

  

Palantir emphasizes keeping customer data under the customer’s control (not copying or selling it). This is a key part of its positioning.

  

4. Platform Approach — Not Just Tools

  

Instead of selling isolated analytics apps, Palantir gives a platform that can be configured and extended into different operational domains (defense, finance, manufacturing, health).

  

5. Controversy & Misunderstanding

  

The company has drawn criticism for privacy and civil liberties concerns — especially when used for things like policing or immigration analysis — but defenders argue its tools don’t predict behavior automatically.

  

  

In a Nutshell – The Palantir Value Proposition

  

What they sell: software platforms for data integration, analytics, and AI-augmented decision systems.

Who uses it: governments, defense, law enforcement, Fortune 500 companies, and enterprises with complex data challenges.

What’s unique: operational focus, deep integrations, secure data control, and the ability to run across environments — from data centers to edge devices.
